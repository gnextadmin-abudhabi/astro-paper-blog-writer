Whether you're a local SEO specialist, a multi-location brand manager, or a small business owner trying to stay ahead of the curve, the first quarter of 2026 delivered no shortage of developments worth paying attention to. From AI reshaping how search actually works to new tools for managing your reputation, Q1 kept the local marketing world busy. Here's a structured roundup of the 20 most important things you need to know.

## AI Learnings for Local Brands

### 1. Google Still Dominates — But the Picture Is More Nuanced Than You Think

SparkToro analyzed 41 websites and found that Google handles approximately 73% of searches — not the widely cited 90%. ChatGPT accounts for just 2.8%. The real takeaway? Google AI Overviews are consuming a massive share of attention within that Google ecosystem. For local brands, this reinforces the importance of an **"everywhere" marketing strategy**. People are searching on Reddit, Instagram, and YouTube, and your brand needs to show up in those conversations too.

### 2. Apple Intelligence Will Run on Google Gemini

Apple confirmed that Apple Intelligence will be powered by Google Gemini — a significant move that further cements Google's dominance across the search landscape. Even as users shift between devices and assistants, the underlying engine points back to Google. Local brands optimizing for Google are, in effect, optimizing for Apple's AI layer as well.

### 3. AI-Driven Mobile Local Packs Are Reshaping the Three-Pack

Research from Sterling Sky and Jepto found that AI-driven mobile local packs now appear for approximately 8% of tracked keywords. These packs show **2 businesses instead of 3**, frequently lack call numbers (causing measurable drops in call volume), don't always align with traditional local pack rankings, and surface roughly one-third as many businesses overall. If your strategy depends on local pack visibility, this warrants close monitoring.

### 4. Query Fan Out Changes the Odds of AI Visibility

AI search doesn't work the way traditional search does. A concept called **query fan out** means AI systems break a single search query into multiple sub-queries, dramatically changing which sources get surfaced. Sterling Sky's webinar featuring Mike King, Rand Fishkin's interviews on the topic, Claire Carlile's accessible explanations, and Mike King's Qforia simulator are all essential resources here. The sobering stat: ranking in the top 10 organically gives you only a **25% chance** of appearing in AI search results. Breadth and authority matter more than ever.

## Google Business Profiles and Local Packs

### 5. Google Published an Official GBP Guide — and the Numbers Are Compelling

Google released an official guide to Google Business Profiles this quarter, and the data inside it should motivate every local business to prioritize their profile. Complete GBPs receive **7x more clicks**. Customers are 29% more likely to consider brands with detailed profiles. Regularly updated profiles earn **5x more views**. If your GBP is incomplete or stale, this is your reminder to fix it.

### 6. A Practical Map to GBP Support

Stefan Somborac documented the current landscape for accessing GBP support — covering reviews, verification issues, and suspensions. Navigating Google's support channels has long been frustrating, and this resource provides a practical reference for the three scenarios local businesses encounter most often.

### 7. Don't Create Separate GBP Listings for Individual Services

Greg Gifford issued a clear warning this quarter: do **not** create separate GBP department listings for services like oil changes or tire rotations. Doing so violates Google's guidelines and risks penalties that could harm your core listing. If you've seen this tactic recommended anywhere, consider it firmly off the table.

### 8. Google Tests 'Text Message' Buttons in Mobile Local Packs

Joy Hawkins spotted Google experimenting with text message buttons inside mobile local packs — and this is genuinely good news. As phone numbers disappear from more interfaces, adding a text-based contact option gives consumers an easy way to reach businesses without requiring a direct call. Watch for this to roll out more broadly.

## Reviews

### 9. Whitespark's Definitive Reputation Management Guide

Whitespark released what may be the most comprehensive guide to local business reputation management published this year. It covers everything: avoiding compliance disasters, earning positive reviews organically, treating reviews as two-way conversations, analyzing sentiment trends over time, fighting review spam, and building long-term reputation moats that competitors can't easily replicate.

### 10. Old Reviews Are Fueling Inaccurate AI Summaries

Claudia Tomina documented a troubling pattern: **3-year-old reviews** are being used to generate AI summaries on Google Business Profiles, even when they no longer reflect a business's current reality. Making matters worse, Google support has not been responsive to mitigation requests. Businesses need to be proactive about generating fresh reviews to counterbalance outdated signals.

### 11. Free Sentiment Analyzer for GBP Reviews

Celeste Gonzales built and released a free GBP Reviews Sentiment Analyzer Chrome extension — a genuinely useful tool for local marketers. It provides exportable data including sentiment summaries, review velocity metrics, and recurring language patterns. Understanding what language your customers naturally use is invaluable for both reputation management and content strategy.

### 12. Google Is Asking Local Guides for Longer, More Detailed Reviews

Google has been emailing Local Guides requesting longer, more detailed reviews. Claudia Tomina's hypothesis is persuasive: Google needs richer training data for its AI tools. Large language models perform better when reviews include specifics about atmosphere, staff interactions, and how problems were resolved. The practical implication? Encourage your reviewers to go beyond star ratings.

### 13. Review Extortion Is Real — Here's How to Fight Back

Joy Hawkins shared actionable strategies for dealing with review extortion this quarter. Google's Merchant Extortion Form exists specifically for these situations, and Joy's team has seen genuine success in getting fraudulent extortion-based reviews removed. If your business has been targeted, document everything and use the official channel.

### 14. AI-Generated Owner Responses Carry Serious Brand Risk

Google tested AI-generated owner responses to reviews — and consumer research should give any brand pause before embracing this feature. **70% of respondents** said they would lose trust in a brand if they discovered AI was generating its review responses. Authenticity in reputation management isn't just a nice-to-have; it's a trust signal with measurable business impact.

## Local Search Marketing Grab Bag

### 15. Google Is Becoming an AI Agent That Shops and Books on Your Behalf

Google is increasingly deploying AI agents capable of calling local businesses to check inventory or confirm reservation availability. Brodie Clark documented this development through Google Merchant Center. We are entering what's being called **"agentic commerce"** — a shift in which AI intermediaries handle parts of the customer journey that previously required human-to-human interaction.

### 16. Press Releases Still Deliver Measurable SEO Value

A Sterling Sky study confirmed that well-executed press releases deliver measurable impacts on both local and organic rankings — and now, on AI search inclusion as well. For local businesses that have dismissed press releases as outdated PR tools, this research makes a compelling case for revisiting that assumption.

### 17. Google LSAs Now Report Spending Breakdowns

Google began providing Local Services Ads spending breakdowns that distinguish between **direct business searches** and lower-funnel queries. This level of transparency is overdue and gives advertisers much better insight into where their LSA budget is actually going — and which query types are driving the most valuable leads.

### 18. Whitespark's Social Media Guide for Local Businesses

Whitespark published a social media guide built specifically for local businesses, drawing on data from SparkToro and GatherUp alongside real-world examples. Rather than generic social media advice, the guide grounds its recommendations in local search behavior and consumer research relevant to community-based brands.

### 19. Optimize Your Contact Page for Both Humans and Crawlers

Darren Shaw's tutorial in Search Engine Land made the case for treating your contact page as a strategic SEO asset. The guidance focuses on three goals: establishing your business identity, building trust signals, and communicating **hyperlocality** — the specific geographic signals that help both consumers and search engines understand exactly where and who you are.

### 20. A Deep Dive on LSA Ranking, Pricing, and Fraud

NearMedia hosted a wide-ranging discussion with Eric Levine (formerly of Google's LSA team), Claudia Tomina, Matt Casady, and Crystal Horton. The conversation covered LSA ranking factors, how pricing actually works inside the platform, and fraud prevention strategies. For any business investing significantly in LSAs, this discussion is required listening.

## The Bigger Picture

Q1 2026 makes one thing unmistakably clear: the speed of AI-driven change in local search is not slowing down. New pack formats, AI-generated summaries, agentic commerce, and shifting ranking mechanisms are all compressing the window local businesses have to adapt.

But amid all the disruption, one principle holds steady: **the businesses that invest genuinely in their local communities — through authentic reviews, real conversations, and meaningful visibility across multiple platforms — will be the ones best positioned to weather whatever comes next**. Digital tactics matter, but they work best when they're rooted in something real.

Stay curious, stay consistent, and keep showing up where your community is already looking.
